Wally

Hello Heather! What a wonderful start of spring - to hear from you and the BBB! I must confess, Natalie and I have just returned from Iron City the weekend of April 23rd. - last weekend - we arrived on the very day the ice opened enough for Gary to pick us up in the cold rain in a little outboard. But, by Saturday and then Sunday afternoon, we kayaked in over 70 degrees to the open to see the still fast ice - paddling though some breaking, crackling and crystalline bays - just beautiful! We've got pictures!

 

Our new cottage is coming along nicely - with all the stars aligned, we'll be in, at least partially, by Memorial Day! We can't wait to show it to everyone - it seems the melding of old and new - all the wonderful things that we all think are great about Iron City - open trusses, water view through trees, close to boardwalk, screened porch entry - we can't wait to get back!

 

Which we will May 7th, then again May 28th, or whatever - that's when we bring in the big truck full of furniture, beds and appliances.

 

As for the BBB, campers should be ready for just that - nothing but bilge water in the back bay. At last report, all back bay docks were indeed floating, but there is shallow water in the approach to them - boaters beware!

 

It will remain to be seen what front docks will be operational - there may be few. We'll see.

 

One reminder to all - especially in advance of the Memorial Day crowd (Gary & Fred - perhaps an early epistle would be in order) - It is absolutely imperative that any members planning on arriving before the opening of camp (and indeed any other time as well) give Gary and Florence as many as three weeks notice that you will be arriving. Otherwise, their priorities for water service, septic pumps, cottage cleaning, docks and the like may not include yours! Please be kind and considerate, and rest assured your needs will be met.

 

This will be a memorable Memorial Day for the Sloans - we wish the same for all ICFC - Rah, Rah, Ray!
