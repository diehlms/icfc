The Collins family

The Collins clan will arrive in camp on July 13 until August 3rd.  This year we are in the Martin cottage.  My brother and his family enjoyed ICFC so much last summer that they will return this summer for the week of July 13th.  My mother Ina Sable will return for her 5th summer. 

 

The Olympics have come and gone from Salt lake City.  What an incredible experience for all of us.  Salt Lake seemed like paradise for 2 weeks.  People could actually walk down the street with a beer in hand!  Both day and evening events were enjoyed by all.  Bare Naked Ladies were fantastic!

 

Russ Harmer is getting back in the swing of things after his stroke September 19th.  He has started back to work and everyday becomes stronger.  Jean has been a god-send through thick and thin. 

 

We all celebrated Anne’s 80th birthday with a trip on the Columbia River. We followed the Lewis and Clark trail for over 400 miles.  Can’t say enough about it.  It was one of the most memorable trips we’ve done as a family.

 

We can’t wait to come to camp, relax, and see all of our friends.

Karen and David Mills

Donald and Lorraine Lawson
Donald and Lorraine had a good winter.  We spent 10 weeks in Naples, Fla.  The sunshine and warm weather really contributed to the recovery and rehabilitation of my broken ankle sustained last August 13th at ICFC. Our time in Florida was interrupted only by a trip to Pittsburgh to attend the wedding of Wally Jenkins and Laura Rittmeyer on February 16th.  It was a beautiful wedding.  We were delighted to be included in such a happy occasion. 

Donald and I have visited ICFC twice in the last 4 months to monitor the progress of our cabin.  The structure, both inside and out, is complete.  Now the challenge is to come to grips with the finishings and furnishing.  It is possible that the cabin will be finished for this summer's encampment. 

We expect to be in camp in July for 2 weeks as well as for the semi-annual meeting.  For those of you who know her, our daughter-in-law Christine ran the Boston Marathon on April 15th and finished in under 4 hrs which was her goal.

The Hugessen Family

The Hugessen family has had a busy year.  How can it not be with three children 6 and under!!  Late last summer I decided it was time to get back into the working world and was very fortunate to get a part-time contract organizing an international conference for this September.  I will lead a delegation of 125 young adults, representing 40 different countries, around Ontario and Quebec for 21 days.  This should prove to the world that Jaime is truly a saint.  I will be abandoning my husband and children for 21 days just at the start of the school year.  While the timing and length of the conference could be better, I have really enjoyed the responsibility and it has been a great way to reintegrate.  My next challenge will be to find contract number two.

 

Richard started at a new school in September and has been thriving.  He has been happy to go to school every day instead of crying and screaming every morning at drop off.  A big relief for Mom!!  Jeffrey is seriously into trucks and construction, especially diggers.  He loves the outdoors and swimming and I know he'll have a blast at ICFC this summer. Michael just celebrated his first birthday and should be walking any day now.  He babbles up a storm and generally has a very pleasant disposition.  Unfortunately sleep deprivation is a problem in our household.

 

Jaime's work life has settled down a little this year after a big merger/integration last year.  He's taken on a few new extra-curricular challenges which will ratchet up the pace again.

 

All in all, we are all well and looking forward to our time at ICFC.  Our new cottage is near completion and we can hardly wait to see it for real, and not just in pictures.

 

Hopefully our paths will cross up north! Cheers,
