The Daily Bilge 2000
  August 24, 2001:
Well it happened....as if in a dream.....George Stronach just came by the weighing station and weighed in a most beautiful bass....and with all the witnesses in camp around (me and Emile) we saw it measure 4lb 2ozes solid.....the smile on his face was as wide as front bay....so I think that declares him winner of the Calhoun for this year.....our deepest condolences to AJ....and with wishes for a great summer of fishing for you all next ... oh boy.....what fun!!!!!!!!!!!!


August 23, 2001:
Well, this is it.....the computer is heading up to Parry Sound to its' winter home so I must also head south....we have had a string of really lovely days....John Helen and Adam (so handsome) Boak are here for one week in camp and then next and I think they really have the time chosen correctly. There has been blue blue blue days....slight chill in the air....the fire ban was lifted cause of the rain over the weekend and we had a bon fire on the beach Tuesday night....I have never seen stars that brilliant or clear....

fishing is unbelievable right now also....yesterday I went with Bruce Lawson and off of Bertos we must of boated 7 bass....I got a nice 3lber I kissed and put back and then a couple of perfectly huge picnic bass...which I will share with the whole camp this morning for breakfast....we are a motley crew in the dining room right now....about 20 after Helen puts on her navy blue travel suit and embarks....for the last week most mornings have been eaten up with good byes....but then I'm happy when I'm on a dock.....

George Stronach has two more days technically to go for the Calhoun Cup himself (members largest SMB) but if he doesn't make it that will go to A.J. Jenkins' SMB she caught off of Bertos.... the Wright Cup will go to Martin Jenkins Jr. for his 4lb 1ozer....and the Kinnear Cup to Jesse Prollo (that's Derek Martin's daughter) for her 3.2........but we do need to give George the 2 days....and he is determined.....so stay tuned...

I would say the summer went pretty well.....really well....and would like to take this opportunity to thank you all for you kindnesses, flexibility and patience....oh yes and humor.....it's a great place isn't it??????

See you next year one way or the other.....Molly


August 16, 2001:
Oh dear....this time of year seems to just slip away for me....can't believe that I haven't gotten back to you all sooner than this.....well! here we go.....the Governor General's visit went very well and very smoothly....just a neighbor's visit really....there was a lot of speculation about what we should wear and what she would wear....and she came in a purple linen skirt and tennis shoes (we  had never guessed that!)
Lovely reception at the Steffey's and an elegant dinner by Emile....she said a few words about sharing the bay with us and her appreciation of the fact that we are good care takers of the environment...an exchange of gifts....dessert out on the front lawn and she was off before a storm was going to break across the bay....

The a quiet weekend really....Porter and Samantha Scott (Gail was busy with the most beautiful and calm Heather) headed up the party and Alexis Lyndsey and Rik Rodefer did the church service....

Last night was the Paddle Dinner which we tried to switch around a bit this year.....we had a bbq for dinner and ice cream sundaes on the front lawn for dessert ....and the Paddle Ceremony by the flag pole....Jason Emond won the paddle this year (this is definitely the year of the Canadians!!!)....and we instituted a new award....called The Anchor.....which went to both Ali Andrews and Ben Johnson for their
outstanding contribution to this year's encampment...

The fishing is amazing right now....we have come out of the weeks of hot weather and into a chill of sorts....you can feel it especially in the morning.....my mother Jake and Sam Morrow decided to go out to Huckleberry for just 45 minutes yesterday before we were leaving for a picnic...and in that time they pulled in 3 nice sized SMB's....great stuff!!!!!

The numbers here are slowly beginning to dwindle....down to 103 today....it's that time of year you spend all morning saying goodbye to people...ugh!!! But on the upside....it is actually supposed to rain today...which everyone is looking forward to (except maybe the Stewarts and Andy Martin who are out camping with their children right now)...but we sure could use the rain and a cabin day will be very welcome!
   -- Molly Schade

August 9, 2001:
Well we are very busy getting ready for Da GG but I can take a few moments to tell you that Charlie Stewart and his daughter Natalie beat Beth Steffey and Bennett Reed for the Tolley Cup this year.....it was very exciting tennis in the finals and there was a huge gallery tucked under the trees out of the sun having a great time....that tournament is really a great part of our summer here. The weather continues to be dry....fire ban still on.....we couldn't cook steaks again this week so we had something called a "Sip and Dip"...we went out to sunset bay, floated and swam off of boats and Moby Dock....we were real rebels and make tomato and onion sandwiches with parsley and pepper added....and they were well received!!!!! Yesterday the artist Lu Robataille came at the invitation of Nick Steffey and it was a very exciting day....she does wonderful watercolors of the Georgian Bay and surrounding areas and many of us will be nailing up one of her paintings on our winter walls to remind us of our summer homes.....then there was a Sing In at the Old Friends Boathouse last night....the 17 part harmonies and laughter could be heard on the Front Bay docks 'til late into the night (I was just happy it wasn't the staff making noise :>) So there you go.....we will be busy this day with the GG's visit today. I will make sure to tell you all about that as soon as possible...
   -- Molly Schade

August 5, 2001:
....just a quick note to report that Joshua Martin....age 6...caught a 5lb 8oz large mouth black bass yesterday....the fish was almost bigger than the child....the fishing is great right now....!!!!!!
   -- Molly Schade


August 4, 2001:
This will be just a quick report.....on meeting day on the 2nd all went very well and smoothly. Lots of meetings and lots of the verb "to porch" afterwards. But on to the important business of this club....which is fishing......yesterday was the Todd Cup and it was a great day for fishing.....there is a very bright moon right now and of course clear and dry weather and a light breeze actually kicked up and so the conditions were just perfect for bass fishing.....there must have been 5 or six lovely stringers of fish up by 5pm....Carol Brammer had a beautiful 3lb on hers.  I believe that Derek Martin Jr. told me on the boardwalk that his daughter Jess got a 3lb2ozer ( the first time she's ever been fishing)..and of course the Fritz brothers were busy weighing fish out of their famous bass bags....but in the end Paul Jenkins prevailed and he will be putting his name on the cup (when John Boak returns it from his last year's victory)....so there you go.... busy week coming up.....on the 8th is the very exciting Tolley Tennis Tournament. And we are going to have a visit from a wonderful local artist Lu Robataille that day....she will be giving a painting class for Arts and Crafts that day and showing some of her works in the Lodge....and then of course even the loons out in front of the McCarthy's house are beginning to show their excitement about the Governor General's visit (I will get excited also when I stop listening to the staff and all the pranks they what to play in the dining room that night....but enough of that....) stay in touch!!!!! Molly
   -- Molly Schade


August 1, 2001:
So much for slow fishing days......Derek Martin Jr. caught a 5lb 2oz large mouth bass off of Whistler's Bay yesterday afternoon. When Sophia Crawford and I were kayaking in from an afternoon on the bay I saw Derek down on the front dock with a sizable fish and we went over there to see it first hand.... Derek was all smiles and told a great story about fishing with his dad and brother in the same hole and that his brother was having a time pulling in some monster fish. It turned out to be a huge ball of seaweed but in the middle of it was this incredible bass (much smaller than 5lbs though)...due to the lack of a net the fish got away. They all laughed about the lack of net and came home with the story. So I asked Derek if he had a net for the 5-lber today.....and he did that Martin chortle laugh and replied "No, no net." He kept the fish alive to show his mom when she arrived right before dinner (and his brother - ) and then bless his soul, released it back into our bay. Yep, this is a fishing camp!!!!!!!!!!!
   -- Molly Schade
 

August 1, 2001:
Oh my the first of August has arrived along with the peak of the summer....weather continues to be lovely though very dry. We had two firefighters down on Monday to talk fire safety and it was very interesting what they had to report. First off there is a fire ban in the area right now...which will of course not alter "the float" but will change the "steak" part of the picnic Friday Night....also they recommended some specific fire emergency procedures which Mike and Gregg will report on at the meeting tomorrow night (so it will be in the minutes of the meeting).

I would call the fishing slow right now. I went out on Moby Dock last night and while waiting for Colleen and Matt's babysitter (and MD to come around the corner) Chris Tadlock and I fished on Schroeder's dock. We caught between us....a pike, a bass, a perch and a sunny. Chris also caught the Kinnears boat but that story should probably not go into print....There was a huge picnic to the Umbrellas yesterday and that might have cut into the regular fishing people's time....sure seems like more pike than in the past....but then again I'm not sure we targeted pike the way we are now....with the lures and plugs...

The Bennetts and the Douglasses had a great Saturday Night Party....with a Survival theme....you had to bring to the party what you could not survive without at Iron City...and then were divided into 2 tribes and the challenges began by age groups....it was a hoot. Dave and Charde tied with Jerry Rampelberg and Chiz Anderson for Better Ball on the putting and the Campers pulled out a win the in the last inning of the softball game. The Brothers Four (Crawfords) did the church service on Sunday which was about the journeys we all make to get up here.....but more specifically theirs that they made with their dad this year. There is a copy of it in the office if you want to take a look at it when you get here.

That's the way it is up here for the last week of July....the Collins all left yesterday....it is always a sad day for all of us.. .and a clear sign that August is here....
   -- Molly Schade
 

July 26, 2001:
Woke up to a big wind this morn pushing right in my mom's front doors (NE) and I came down and lit a fire in the dining room first thing...a pretty far cry from the heat of a couple of days ago...one thing about the Georgian Bay...if you don't like the weather you are in luck...it changes so regularly....

In fishing news....Sam Raley brought in a beautiful 3lb SMB a day or so ago....he was taking a break from catching snakes and scaring Meg Schade with them....and here's a REAL news flash....John Andrews (of the Reed family...not necessarily know for their long expertise in fishing)....hung up a 2lb10oz lovely the same day....I didn't see the fish last night cause there was a steak picnic...windy evening perfect steak night....Jenifer Raley and I decided we want to bring the kids out to a rock one night soon...just so they can sit around a fire, hear the loons and watch the sun go down the way we did last night.....

Coming up on Monday is our big Fire Safety Day....the Fire and Safety Committee (and the Insurance one also) have two fire fighters/consultants coming into camp...they are going to take a walk about and then have a round table discussion about how to create and maintain the safest camp possible....it should be very interesting and certainly pertinent in relation to the fact we have had so little rain this year....it is so dry here...wait 'til you see the brown moss and dried up bushes everywhere you go...so that's July 30th...we will all learn a lot I am sure.

We are all preparing for the slow build towards...hope you are all busy packing up and planning for the best year ever!!!!!!
   -- Molly Schade
 

July 20, 2001:
Well the high wind and cold temperatures seems like a dream from ages gone by....we have hit a spell of warm days with soft winds....this usually happens when the Sloans are in town.....it was a picture perfect day for the Collins Cup on Wednesday...and there was a lot of participation this year but George Stronach prevailed over the group once more and was the winner.....Emile was cleaning fish 'til late into the night and there were no picnics yesterday because there were so many people having fish for lunch....!!!

We had an adult kayak trip yesterday in the morning....I got to lead it and we took a loop up by Baldy. past a bay my family calls "Yo Man"...past Mary Kinnear's Rock, over thro Pershings Channel (I was checking out new shoals for a bunch of sacred Steffey fishing holes on the way) There are many beaver in Pershing's Bay this year. We saw their homes but not them. Going thro Indian Channel I saw the first Cardinal flowers in bloom of the year (which is a serious sign of August in my book) and the largest extended family of Canadian geese I've ever seen up here....must have been forty of them all ages......all honking away and feeding in the tall grass....they looked like Iron City folk on the way to the dining room.....

A group of us sat on the Fritz porch last night.....sipping a little cordial which seems, once a year, to be a Tolley tradition....and watched an incredible sunset...which was orchestrated by 2 loons that were swimming around Pikes Peak....and the voices of the staff and camp children on the bagel.....my, my, my......what a sight......when is it you people are going to get up here?.....you are missing all the fun!!!!!!!
   -- Molly Schade


July 17, 2001:
Dear ICFC Members,

The Canadian Governor General, The Honorable Adrienne Clarkson and her husband John Ralston Saul a noted philosopher, author and academic have accepted our invitation to visit IC in August. These neighbors live in a cabin on one of the nearest islands to ICFC (to the right of Pike Peak). They will visit on either the 9th or 10th of August. David Mills forwarded a copy of Adrienne's acceptance speech. This presentation will give you insight into the life and views of one of Canada's most visible and global dignitaries. This Speach provides a wonderful historical perspective.

You will learn much about the Canadian heritage and spirit.

Regards,

Chip

July 15, 2001:
Well it happened....I guess I knew it would at some point but it is such an amazing sensation ....woke up this morning and the wind was gone....flat calm in the front bay on this Sunday morn....I saw a couple of kayaks going across the bay at about 6:30am....think I might do the same later.....but this is the first morning in a long while with no wind....there has been lots of fishing in the North Channel (to get out of the wind) and certainly a lot of sailing and windsurfing (Monty is in town...Ted Branch would have been out there if he hadn't broken his elbow the day before he came up) Yesterday a big group of people went over to Camp Hurontario to see an art exhibit by a woman named Lou Robataille. Lou is going to be here in camp showing her artwork on August 8th...I think you will all be amazed to see what she does and want to have it in your homes (north and south). Big Saturday Night Party last night....the Hugessons and Bruce Lawson ran an Olympic Evening because of the fact Toronto came in second for the summer Games of 2008.. Was it..China that won???.... You were supposed to come as your favorite foreign country so that the grand march could be a parade of nations....I must admit though, I was very proud of how the Staff2K1 got involved and all dressed up (Jason and Al were from EmileLand in the white kitchen hats and aprons)....it was little Eleanor Burcham about 1 year old in a kimono and a sign WE WON on her back that stole my heart. Hopefully more fish to report next time .....

P.S.:
I know I am always asking you all why you are not up here....but at this writing what I am curious about is: when ARE you coming? I just checked the books and right now we have 72 reservations for the night of the annual meeting and that seems somewhat low to me....so come on all you august people we need you to begin to think about your dates and make those ever precious reservations so we can plan for the onslaught.....I mean for your vacation!!!!!!!! Email, FAX (we got it working finally) or call ....we are here in the office most mornings and would love to hear from YOU!!!!!!!
 

July 11, 2001:
I realize that it has been a while....we have all been sort of in a daze since Darrin's accident and death. I hope all of you that knew him will be able to get his parent's address soon and will be able to write a letter to them. The service is tomorrow and I will get a report and let you all know what I find out.

The wind has been very high all week....but the fishing steady and good....my mother and I found a lovely little bay out of the wind the other day and she brought in a great looking bass which we had for breakfast this morn but more importantly we lost an AMAZING one that we will work the summer to see again....like on Todd Cup day.....Stephanie Nuel went out with the Jenkins today and brought in a well over 2lber...I think 2lb13oz...but I not sure....I sat next to her at dinner and she was beaming!!!!

We had our first steak picnic on Monday....with the mighty number of 21 we had a great breeze that kept the bugs away all night and with that number the steaks and potatoes were cooked to perfection....I saw Jaime Hugesson pour a goodly amount of red wine in the potatoes and will admit they were VERY different and would recommend this for the rest of the year.....

We had a tennis tournament on Saturday, singles junior style. Alex Johnson won but had a hard time of it against Daniel Modic who has the making of a great tennis player.....Stan Williams and Patty Steffey won the golf on Sunday....

So there you go....again it is very cold and very windy.  Pack lots of sweatshirts when you come though keep in mind we have some green, blue, wine color ones in the office this year with a Georgian Bay tree and ICFC in them...Jim had them done up and they are selling like hot cakes right now.....half the camp is out of electricity tonight cause of a major Hydro problem and it is wonderful walking up and down the boardwalk seeing everyone’s candles and kerosene lamps lit inside...I am off to play a game of Murder in the Dark right now .....why is it you aren't here??????
 

July 6, 2001:
The fourth was fabulous here.....we had a real break in the weather and we were all  out on the beach having a grand time.....there were 5 entries for the sand castle contest....each amazing and fun...one even had a bull frog named Molly and lily pads....it of course won a special award..then again they all won awards....then s'mores brought in by the Burkes...little frozen buckets of adult quenchers by Colleen McCarthy and Debbie Cerskus and the fire works off of Moby Dock that went on forever.....VERY special and much thanks to all involved....

Then on Thursday we had an early morning storm that blew our transformer here at the office and the kitchen...much excitement around the fact we had to try to be flush free for the day to take the pressure off the generator and our sewage system...but by the end of the day Hydro was here and a helicopter flew in our new transformer and the lights magically went on during dinner....the sound of flushing toilets could be heard across the bay all night!!!

Still cold today but the sun came out.  They did a really cute project in Arts and Crafts this morning...wonderful sand paintings that our A&C person...Ali Andrews organized and then hung all over the dining room....Helen Runnells Dubois and her son Pierre caught a 22lb catfish...by reports the fish was longer than Pierre.....and our headwaiter Melissa Milanak arrived finally safe and sound tonight....so all is well...quiet bay tonight...I almost miss the wild wind we've had but I guess that is because of all the mosquitoes in the office...so why is it you all aren't here?????

July 4, 2001:
News flash news flash new flash.....Marten Jenkins Jr. caught a 4lb 1 oz bass yesterday....and when asked in the dining room in front of the 122 people having dining last night...where he caught it...his response was a surprising "out there."....there are fish everywhere this year....the McCarthys fish every afternoon and say there are lot of pike and bass from the camp up to Baldy....we have a new fish scale this year from I believe Bruce Lawson...I hope to get it up some time this week before his arrival....

 

We are to have rain for the next 5 days....but Happy 4th to you all...we are going to have a big waterfront night tonight if the rain breaks...sand castle contest, volleyball and big all camp relay involving running, swimming, kayaking, and the last leg is running up the beach and ringing the welcome bell. The Burke's have supplied the s'mores, the McCarthy's and the Cerskus' have supplied huge buckets of daiquiris (don't tell anyone) and the big even of the night is fireworks off of Moby Dock that the McCarthy's brought up....this is BIG and wish you were here.......
   -- Molly Schade
 

July 2, 2001:
Well, so much for the flat calm...the wind kicked up big time as a front blew through and we had HUGE white caps on the front bay and every body digging in their closets for warm clothes and blankets... we had a BIG pine tree go over in front of Yount's cabin and a lot of minor dock damage.  The dock out beyond Reeds actually blew away..it was too wet to putt but the softball was great without the usual attending bugs of one sort or another and the STAFF2K1 blew the campers away (well, it was 8-7)...still big wind today but the regular regimen of picnics are going out anywho...

Val King got the first bass of the season as bass season opened Saturday...1lb16oz SMB and the Modics had a bass and a pike on the board before their bags were off the dock....Brandon McCarthy was passing around his pike in the dining room last night which was of course the best thing I had eaten in a LONG while...

There was a Crispy Crunch Tournament on the front dock this morning...which my brother Nick and passed as we were trying out the newly renovated Skipper. (Which is in my humble opinion the most beautiful boat in camp right now)..looked like the whole camp was there...and by the way the dining room is almost filled (not the porch)...our count is 111 right now...which is polishing up the staff dining room routine nicely....
   -- Molly Schade
 

June 29, 2001:
Well, it happened...camp opened for dinner on Wednesday night...we had a record crowd and leg of lamb...We celebrated Helen Wright's 58th encampment and then Rah Rah Rayed Eleanor Heath's 70th!!!! We have had a string of days that have been incredibly beautiful...some folks have dared to complain about the heat but Barbara Boucek and I were both out on the benches by the beach just celebrating the breeze and it being so comfortable...the staff is great this year....they had a great AOT (Adventure Orientation and Training), and are pinning every meal in the dining room.

CJ Sloan and the book keeper Tom Alphin (a Bucknell friend of CJ's who is really fun....stop by the office when you get into town and introduce yourself to him) have set up what I refer to as Mission Control in the Chief's room for the staff only...5 computers, a burner for music CD's, scanner and DVD capabilities with 151 movies to keep little staff minds happy at night...

Emile has a really young staff in this year in the new Hilton on the Hill ..stop by there anytime...they will love to show you around......No fish to report but then again Clive just got into town last night and I'm planning on getting my license today. We are slowly finding out about the phones here in camp, a lot of them were blown out by the storm over Memorial Day. If you have turned your phone off for the winter....it would be helpful for you to turn it on now so you can see it works....let us know what we can do to help!!!!!!

I know I say this every time.....but I don't know why you all are down there....we have a flat calm morning which would be perfect for a kayak, water ski or fishing those hole that are hard to get the anchor to hold in the wind....bad day for the Moon but I was wondering about the Westerns...perfect Westerns day
   -- Molly Schade
 

May 4, 2001:
 

The Boaks report an out-of-season record catch for John and Helen! A 7 lb 10 oz small mouth baby Boak measuring 20 inches in length!

This little guy has been big news on the Boak front. With John at Helen's side, Adam Michael Boak was born April 25, 2001 at 8:27 pm in Bethlehem, PA, ten days earlier than the 2001 solar/lunar tables predicted. Adam is a blessing and John & Helen are just thrilled to embark on their lifelong journey of parenting.

Helen plans to return to work in July as John will take on the full-time role of being Mr. Mom, but not before he makes his first visit to ICFC when camp opens. Then, at the end of August, the Boak threesome will head north for Adam's first ICFC visit. Rah, rah, ray... three Boaks on the bay!

Helen Boak  (E-Mail Helen and John)
