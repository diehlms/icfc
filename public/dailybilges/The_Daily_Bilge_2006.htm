August 21, 2006:
I have a suspicion that this may be the last entry for the year, since there is only Brian Sloan left on the leader team and a mere 5 waiters. This sunny Monday morning is what we dream of when we think of the end of camp at Iron City - bright sunshine, a cool 60 degrees, a light breeze, and forecast of 73 and sun this afternoon. Heaven on earth.

After what was a very sad week in the history of Iron City, things crept back to normal as fine weather and great friends greeted each day. The Paddle Dinner was a huge success with the finest food ever served, and the winner was announced - congratulations to Alexis Rodefer! You might appreciate the attached poem by Brian Sloan (edited extensively).

The campers won the last ball game of the season with Bobby Reigeluth at the mound - a huge comeback in the final innings with a win by one run. As a gesture of great thanks for all their hard work this year, campers cleared the dinner plates for staff anyway.

We all arrived at breakfast this morning to hear the tale of two rock bass, six small frogs and one bullfrog that had somehow found their way into the drinking water cooler reservoir in the kitchen overnight - mother nature works in strange ways - Chef John found the lid askew and the two rock bass, but no frogs were to be seen, until later when the bullfrog alone was found in the corner of the pantry. Can anyone solve the mystery of the fate of the small frogs?

The orange juice tasted OK to me. Others weren't so certain.

The kids emptied out the store this morning - small amounts of candy and soda were made available to all for free - there's another reason to stay until the end of camp.

On behalf of our leadership team - Brian, Rory, David, Devon and Maggie - Thanks to everyone for helping to make this a wonderful summer for all of us. We'll see you next year on the Bay, and hopefully elsewhere in the meantime.

Signing off for ICFC,

Wally

August 5, 2006:
Hello IRON CITY!

Camp is getting full ladies and gentleman so get your reservations in while you can! We may not have any “limited time only” offers or complimentary day-care, but there are put-put tournaments and some fishing to be had so get up here before it’s too late! We had a torrential rain fall this past week—well it wasn’t really a rainfall, it pretty much flew sideways as if fired from a mini-gun by Arnold Schwarzenegger. But Arnold won’t be back and the weather has turned for the much better, so no excuses any more for shy campers such as yourselves to not be hopping on a plane or in a car and coming up to the bay.

Well now that you’ve been properly warned, here is the news from the far north:

This past Tuesday we had our first ever Wooden Boat Regatta complete with Picnic and sunset cruise for wooden boats of all shapes and sizes. It was pretty amazing to see the range of vessels out on the water with a rowboat from the turn of the century and an outboard from 1926 all the way up to inboards from the 1990’s. A big round of applause to Bobby Reigaluth, George Milligan, and Greg Scott for their organization of the event.

Just yesterday we had the 2006 Todd Cup, awarded to the fisherperson with the largest stringer of small-mouth bass, and after many competitive entries ranging from illegally fished stringers to a small spotted turtle, David Fritz was the wire to wire victor with 6lbs of bass by the end of the day.

Well that brings us to today with the opening rounds of the Tolley Tennis Tournament starting this morning, we’ll be sure to update you all on the winner.

Lastly, are you looking for something to wear to a tennis tournament? A country club? The US Open? What about something to hide the Iron City beer gut? Well sitting next to me in the Iron City office are piles and piles of 125th anniversary Golf-Shirts in Navy and White and we have all sizes from small to extra large so grab one while you can. If you aren’t going to be here ‘till near the end of camp, give us a call at 705-375-2300 and we’ll set on aside for you!

That’s all the news from the bay! We’ll keep you posted.’

ICFC Leadership Team

July 26, 2006:
Hello Campers, here is what news we have from the bay;

As part of our quasquincentenial celebrations, Iron City will have its first ever wooden-boat regalia led by Greg Scott, George Milligan, and Bob Reigeluth. The even will be on Tuesday August 1st with an assembly on the main dock and beach, and we will have a picnic (sign-ups start July 29th). The fleet itself will include all wooden inboards, outboards, canoes, sailing canoes, rowboats and sailboats.

Hope to see you all there!

Just last night we continued our weekly evening of storytelling this time with Anne Collins, Jim Kinnear and Charde Bennett, the burden of performance was not on them entirely as there were plenty of contributions and stories related from the attending campers. Everyone got to hear each of their individual stories whether they concerned Jim and Mary’s early romance to Charde Bennett’s knife fight with Burt Todd. We hope to see this success continued every Wednesday evening till the end of the summer.

That’s all the news from the bay!

July 19, 2006:
Hello Hello Iron City Campers, we have a bunch of dates needing to be saved comin’ up in the next few weeks.

On Saturday, the 22nd there will a silent auction for the Georgian Bay Land Trust, all proceeds go to the GBLT.

This Monday, the 24th is the Tom Collins Conservation Cup presided over by Anne Collins.

The following Friday, the 28th is the Sundowner on the Rocks at San Souci. The cost is $75 a plate and benefits the Georgian Bay Foundation Wetlands Preservation. Details are in the Dining Hall and Office.

Greg Scott will also be leading a nature walk on the Iron City Trail Monday, July 31st. Biologists Chris Haxel and Rick Sneider will be accompanying the group to identify the plant life along the trail.

Finally, the weekly storytelling event is now officially every Wednesday night. Our first evening was led by Barbara Boucek and featured a large group of campers, Chip Crawford, Lee Alexander, Eric Williams, Alex Scott, Cynnie Anderson, Eric Williams, and Jim Beatty. Stories of old Staff shenanigans and romances were discussed, the details of which caused much controversy. This past Wednesday had Anne Collins with the help of Greg Scott digging through old Archives to show us what camp was like in the 1930’s as well as shedding light on the details of our first few encampments.

That’s the news from the bay we hope to see you all here soon. We’ll be in the Office from 8am-noon, 1-2pm and 7-8pm and have all the details regarding any upcoming events.

July 15, 2006:
The wind is picking up here on Georgian Bay after several days of stifling stillness and the flies are being routed from camp, for the time being at least. Numbers are in the mid-July low and not one member of the Vespers committee is here so it looks like God shall go unworshiped this week unless we find someone to lead us.

Last night we had our first steak picnic of the season and not one camper or guest was apprehended by the OPP!! A hearty success for all. We will also be having a beach day for all in camp this coming Tuesday the 18th. We decided to host a beach day in order to stir up some envy in all of you campers not with us at Iron City.

Speaking of envy, Clive Runnells has created quite a bit with his recent strings of fish, we have two staff members trying to discover his secrets by stalking him in a canoe, we will keep you all posted.

White and Navy anniversary Golf shirts are still on sale here in the office. If you need to reach us in the office you can just pick up the phone, our office hours are 8-12am, 1-2pm and 7-8pm. If you cannot reach us during these hours then our bookkeeper is checking the email, at least one of us is always in the office during these times.

July 6, 2006:
Our 125th Independence Day has come and gone on the bay and camper numbers begin to dwindle. Here in the office we still have a good number of anniversary polo’s for sale and be sure to grab a pocket knife while you can.

On Monday we had a ping-pong tournament with 18 contestants battling for a Tervis Tumbler containing a crispy crunch. One of our own staffers, Zach Martin, mopped the floor of the lodge with an assortment of Sagers, Pattons, and Chip Crawford to win in a 21-17 victory in the final round. The 4th of July was beset with a small tempest after breakfast but volleyball and sandcastle building went on in the afternoon with nothing less than gorgeous summer weather. Though the competition was informal, the staff’s Team Domination emerged victorious against the camper team. Anything you may or may not have heard regarding a camper victory in softball last Sunday is unsubstantiated hearsay. Finally Monty Scott and Dr. Laham conquered our Doubles Tennis tournament yesterday morning also winning a Tervis Tumbler containing a crispy crunch.

The 4th of July featured a grand fireworks display by the Jenks out on Jenks Island and Monty Scott on Pikes Peak. Monty is also accepting optional compensation for the cost of the fireworks.

Speaking of giving money, I would like to invite all campers to contribute a pittance (or more if you wish) to the IC staff fund, the money goes to various staff activities like fishing, tubing, staff trip to Parry Sound, or dinner at Henry’s fish restaurant. Otherwise the staff gets bored and starts setting things on fire, we must be appeased. Any donation is appreciated.

That’s all the news from the Bay we hope to see you soon,

ICFC Staff

June 24, 2006:
A quasquicentennial comes only so often, about every 125 years or so. It is an anniversary that most of us only get to experience once in our lifetimes and it is upon us today. It has been an amazing first day up on the bay now that Iron City’s 125th encampment has officially commenced, and on behalf of the Staff and Leadership of the Iron City Fishing Club, we beckon to those who still remain south of the bay to join us for the summer.

125 is going to be a special summer for many reasons not the least of which is the multitude of Camper-Led Initiatives (CLIs) taking place throughout the summer to celebrate our 125th anniversary. Ideas for Iron City’s quasquicentennial (quah-ski-sen-ten-ee-ul) can be emailed to nicksteffey@yahoo.com.

In addition to the CLIs we will have commemorative 125th anniversary pocketknives available in the office for $10 each, hurry up because there are a limited number and they undoubtedly will be coveted as collectibles worth millions in the years to come.

We hope for this to be a wonderful summer for you all with an exciting mix of new improvements with a return to traditional camp activities. Remember when dessert was home cooked right in the dining hall? Or when canoes were not merely relaxing mode of transportation but a trusty steed suitable for aquatic jousting? You may not remember all of the old-timey traditions of yester-year, but we hope that the commemorative activities planned for each and every week this season will enhance and enrich all of our experiences up here at Iron City.

Summer 2006 will be very special for one other reason: our new chef. John Saunders (the Colonel) of Sodexho catering will be serving the camp this year and we on the staff could not be more excited. Us waiters and office folk have been fed daily by the wonderful kitchen staff while training for opening, and after polishing through the homemade apple crumble and whipped cream we gave a rousing two thumbs up to the Colonel. We are confident you will enjoy how they have “raised the bar” in the Dining Hall. We are fortunate to have leather punchers included in the new commemorative pocketknives for adding a few extra holes to our belts.

The team leaders and the Head Chef have been busy training the ICFC wait staff over the last few days and as a final coup de théâtre the staff attempted a canoe trip and campout. The evening was a success despite the late night flight back to camp with a thunderstorm biting at our heels. The staff was assured that it was a bonding experience.

A few bits of business, Gary would like to remind us that it is especially important for children to be kept from playing on or around the water tower area, it is very easily broken and can become dangerous. Also a variety of ICFC clothing is still available for purchase in the office and phonebooks are available on request.
Look forward to seeing you on the bay!

ICFC Staff
Headwaiters, Maggie Martin and Devon Sawin.
Office Folk, Rory J Kinnear, David Scott, Brian Sloan.

Winterfest 2006 Photos from Chip Crawford

February 18, 2006:

Fourteen intrepid campers ventured to IC for Winterfest VII. We met the Beattys for dinner and a Barrie Colts hockey game Thursday Feb 9th arriving on the bay Friday morning (on snow mobiles rented from Muskoka Proline in Port Carling). Wally, CJ and Rik arrived a day early to warm up the camp, and provision the cabins for the arrival of the main group. Cabins in use were: Rodefer, Lawson, Sloan and W. Crawford.

Nick and Fred drove from Boston with a complete New England Clam Bake (fresh lobster, lobster bisque, and clams). Other facets of the trip included curling at the MacTier Curling rink, dinner at the Crane Lake Lodge and a wintry picnic campfire lunch the open side of Green Island.

Bryan Phillips was able to visited the beautifully renovated Sipe (now Phillips) cabin with the camp architect extraordinaire, Gary Johnson. Bob Boucek and Gary walked the Boucek site where the cabin foundations are now planted firmly in the Precambrian shield. (Bob will be talking with Barbara about clearing low vegetation to be sure there is a clean view of the Audrey and Sandy Phillips Memorial Shed.)

Jaime Hugesson is vying for the IC extreme sports award as he was sighted skiing behind a fast moving snow mobile operated by Ted Branch.

CJ and Ted constructed a massive 18 foot bonfire on the ice our last night. The fire was still burning the following morning to welcome a new snow fall - picture camp dusted with white powder from the ground to the top of the trees..............

The Gregiores were with us for most activities and were one of the best support teams you can imagine.....keeping us on the trail and out of harms way.

Soon the Spring will descend and camp will awaken to the sound of returning wildlife, and wild flowers; busy workers and laughter. Camp vitality depends on 'camper days' so plan your family Iron City vacation to enjoy as many days on the bay as you can arrange.

Rah Rah Ray !!!
